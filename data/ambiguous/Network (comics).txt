'''Network''' is the name of three unrelated [[fictional character]]s in the [[Marvel Comics]] [[Marvel Universe|universe]]. One is a teleporting villain, the others are both [[Superhero#Female superheroes|superheroine]]s with the power to communicate with and/or manipulate machines.

==Network (Hellbent)==
A teleporting member of the group of [[Moon Knight]] foes known as the Hellbent.

'''Network''' ('''Sarah Vale<ref name="New X-Men: Yearbook Special #1">''New X-Men: Academy X Yearbook Special'' #1 (2005)</ref>''') is a [[fictional character]], a mutant who appears in the [[Marvel Comics]] series ''[[New X-Men|New X-Men: Academy X]]'' as a student at the [[X-Mansion|Xavier Institute for Higher Learning]]. She is the sister of fellow student [[Preview (comics)|Preview]].

===Fictional character biography===
Not much is known about the mutant known as Network. She is voted most likely to be a millionaire after graduation.<ref name="New X-Men: Yearbook Special #1"/> She is one of the many mutants who lose their powers on [[Decimation (comics)|M-Day]], and she is killed on the bus of depowered former Xavier Institute students that is destroyed by agents of [[William Stryker]].<ref>''New X-Men'', vol. 2 #23 (2006)</ref>

===Powers and abilities===
Before she was depowered, Sarah possessed [[List of comic book superpowers#Technopathy|technopathy/cyberpathy]]: she could interact with technology at an intimate level, making them follow her commands such as overriding security, starting up computers and manipulating programming. The extent of her powers was unrevealed.<ref name="New X-Men: Yearbook Special #1"/>

'''Network''' ('''Valerie Martin''') is a fictional [[superheroine]] from the [[Marvel Comics]] universe. She first appeared in ''Civil War: Front Line.'' and was created by [[Paul Jenkins (writer)|Paul Jenkins]] and [[Ramon Bachs]]

===Fictional character biography===
During the [[Civil War (comic book)|Civil War]] event, a third character using the name Network was among the anti-Registration Act faction of heroes, and became involved with a group of like-minded heroes. She tried to use her powers to locate [[Captain America]] and his Secret Avengers to join them, but didn't have enough time as she and the other members of the gang were apprehended by [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]]{{issue|date=January 2013}}

Network has been identified as one of the 142 registered superheroes who are part of the 50-State Initiative.<ref>[[Avengers: The Initiative]] #1</ref> <ref>[http://www.marvel.com/news/comicstories.947 Avengers: The Initiative #1 Character Map]</ref>
{{-}}

==Other characters==
* Wayne Tucker of the [[New Universe]] was referred to by the code-name "Network" in a file in ''[[Psi-Force]]'' Annual #1, but never adopted the name for himself.

==Other versions==
===Ultimate Network===
* In the ''[[Ultimate Marvel]]'' reality, a version of Network appears in the series ''[[Ultimate Comics: X-Men]]''. This version is named '''Seth Vale''' and is a former mutant prisoner of Camp: Angel, and joined the resistance led by [[Kitty Pryde]]. <ref>''Ultimate Comics: X-Men'' #19</ref>

==Other media==
===Television===
* The Sarah Vale version of Network appears in the ''[[Wolverine and the X-Men (TV series)|Wolverine and the X-Men]]'' episode "X-Calibre" voiced by [[Grey DeLisle]].<ref>''Wolverine e os X-Men (Wolverine and the X-Men)''</ref> She is shown on a ship to Genosha which is raided by Spiral and the Reavers. She helps in keeping the ship from sinking by communicating with the ship.
