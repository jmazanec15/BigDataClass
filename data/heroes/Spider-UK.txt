'''Spider-UK''' is a British-based Spider-Man appearing in [[Marvel Comics]]. He is notable for being of one of the lead characters in "[[Spider-Verse]]" storyline and is the leader of the [[Web Warriors]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Spider-Man: 28 Marvel Superheroes With Spider-Powers, Ranked|url=https://screenrant.com/spider-man-marvel-superheroes-with-spider-powers-ranked/|website=Screen Rant|accessdate=4 January 2018|date=17 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Who Are All These Spider-People Slinging Around Marvel Comics?|url=https://www.inverse.com/article/15689-who-are-all-these-spider-people-a-guide-to-marvel-s-current-webslingers|website=Inverse|accessdate=4 January 2018|language=en}}</ref>

Spider-UK first appeared in ''Edge of Spider-Verse'' #2 and was created by [[Jason Latour]] and Robbi Rodriguez.

==Fictional character biography==
Originating from Earth-833, Billy Braddock is one of the newest recruits of the Captain Britain Corps who operates as Spider-UK. Scanning other universes during the "[[Spider-Verse]]" storyline, he has seen the deaths of alternate versions of [[Spider-Man]] by the Inheritors. [[Morlun]] slays [[Spider-Man and His Amazing Friends|Earth-1983 Spider-Man]] and his teammates [[Iceman (Marvel Comics)|Iceman]] and [[Firestar]]. [[List of Marvel Comics characters: B#Bora|Bora]] and [[List of Marvel Comics characters: B#Brix|Brix]] arrive on Earth-999 and kill Spider-Cat. On Earth-7831, [[List of Marvel Comics characters: D#Daemos|Daemos]] kills Spider-Man and several of the [[New Men (Marvel Comics)|animalistic residents]] of [[Counter-Earth (comics)|Counter-Earth]]. Daemos' brother [[List of Marvel Comics characters: J#Jennix|Jennix]] detects Billy watching them. He immediately cuts the scanner feed. As [[Saturnyne]] and [[Roma (comics)|Lady Roma]] discuss their concerns about the Incursions that are destroying many dimensions, Spider-UK tries to explain about the killings of the Spiders across the multiverse. Saturnyne rebuffs him, but a sympathetic Roma gives him a talisman that allows him to travel through the web of life in order to save the remaining Spiders.<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' vol. 3 #7 (2014)</ref> Spider-UK was first seen having arrived in Earth-65 where he sees [[Spider-Woman (Gwen Stacy)|Spider-Woman]] as a possible recruit for the army of Spider-Men.<ref>''Edge of Spider-Verse'' #2</ref> Spider-UK later accompanies [[Spider-Woman (Jessica Drew)|Spider-Woman]], [[Anya Corazon|Spider-Girl]], [[Spider-Man 2099]], [[Spider-Girl|Spider-Girl of Earth-982]], and [[Spider-Ham]] into meeting with Spider-Man and [[Silk (comics)|Silk]]. Spider-UK explains they are the Spiders of other dimensions and that all the strands of the Great Web are converging on Earth-616 Peter. Spider-UK tells him Morlun's elder brother Daemos is coming to Earth-616 and all of them head into a portal to another dimension. When on Earth-13, Spider-Man is told by Spider-UK and Earth-13's Spider-Man who never lost the [[Captain Universe|Enigma Force]] that there is a war coming and that Spider-Man is the greatest Spider-Man of all.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' vol. 3 #9 (2014)</ref> It is explained by Spider-UK and Spider-Man of Earth-13 that Spider-Man is the only Spider-Man that faced an Inheritor and won. Spider-UK gets a reading on the second team and Old Man Spider-Man of Earth-4 says they need to act quickly to recruit them.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' vol. 3 #10 (2014)</ref> When Morlun and his father [[List of Marvel Comics characters: S#Solus|Solus]] attack Earth-13, Spider-UK calls Spider-Man and tells him the Safe Zone is lost.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' vol. 3 #12 (2014)</ref> Kwaku Anansi of Earth-7082 is a spider totem that is recruited by Spider-UK. Before he can join, Anansi needs to trick a demi-god Mister Mighty after stealing his sheep, then escape from Shungo the storm god. On Earth-TRN521, Spider-UK recruits the Mexican Spider-Man named Arácnido.<ref>''Spider-Verse'' #2</ref> On Earth-3123, Karn is stopped from feeding on Spider-Ma'am by Spider-Girl, Spider-UK, Spider-Punk, [[Spider-Man: India|Spider-Man of India]], and [[Spider-Girl#Old Man Logan|Spider-Woman of Earth-807128]].<ref>''Spider-Verse Team-Up'' #3</ref> Spider-UK became a sole survivor from Earth-833 after his home reality was destroyed during the ''[[Time Runs Out]]'' stortyline and chose to remain in the reality of the defeated Inheritors along with Anya Corazon.<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' vol. 3 #15 (2015)</ref>

During the second volume of ''[[Spider-Verse]]'' set during the ''[[Secret Wars (2015 comic book)|Secret Wars]]'' event, Spider-UK and Anya Corazon found themselves in the [[Battleworld]] domain of Arachnia with no memories of how they got there.<ref>''Spider-Verse'' vol. 2 #1 (May. 2015)</ref> They eventually teamed up with Spider-Woman of Earth-65, Spider-Ham, [[Spider-Man Noir]], and Spider-Man of India with neither of them remembering their previous encounter during the original ''Spider-Verse''. After Gwen Stacy meets with the other spider-based heroes after her visit to [[Oscorp]], they are interrupted by the arrival of the [[Sinister Six]] (consisting of [[Doctor Octopus]], [[Electro (Marvel Comics)|Electro]], [[Kraven the Hunter]], [[Sandman (Marvel Comics)|Sandman]], [[Mac Gargan|Scorpion]], and [[Vulture (Marvel Comics)|Vulture]]).<ref>''Spider-Verse'' vol. 2 #2 (Jun. 2015)</ref> The Sinister Six defeat the Spider-Men and bring them to Mayor [[Norman Osborn]] who states he wants to help them. This causes Spider-UK and the other Spider-Men to linger around Oscorp for awhile.<ref>''Spider-Verse'' vol. 2 #3 (2015)</ref> Spider-UK and the other Spider-Men join Spider-Woman of Earth-65 and Arachnia's Spider-Man into defeating Norman Osborn and the Sinister Six.<ref>''Spider-Verse'' vol. 2 #5 (2015)</ref>

Following the conclusion of ''Secret Wars'' the team of six Spiders will rename itself and feature in a new ongoing series called ''Web Warriors'', a name that was coined by Peter Parker from the [[Ultimate Spider-Man (TV series)|''Ultimate Spider-Man'' TV series]] during the original ''Spider-Verse''.<ref name="Web Warriors">{{cite web |first=Rich |last=Johnston |authorlink=Rich Johnston |url=http://www.bleedingcool.com/2015/06/29/spider-gwen-stars-in-web-warriors-launched-by-mike-costa-and-david-baldeon-marveloctober/ |title=Spider-Gwen Stars In Web Warriors Launched By Mike Costa And David Baldeon #MarvelOctober (UPDATE) |publisher=[[Bleeding Cool]] |date=29 June 2015 |accessdate=29 August 2015|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304001923/http://www.bleedingcool.com/2015/06/29/spider-gwen-stars-in-web-warriors-launched-by-mike-costa-and-david-baldeon-marveloctober/|archivedate=March 4, 2016}}</ref> 

==Powers and abilities==
Spider-UK has powers similar to Spider-Man which include sticking and climbing on walls, a sixth sense to danger–which he struggles with–and being able to lift approximately 10 tons. He also has his own web-shooters.

Spider-UK also makes use of an inter-dimensional device that enables him to travel to different dimensions.
