'''Montana''' ('''Jackson W. Brice''') is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Montana first appeared in ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man]]'' #10 (March 1964), and was created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[Steve Ditko]].

The character subsequently appears in ''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #14 (July 1964), #19 (December 1964), ''The Amazing Spider-Man Annual'' #1 (1964), ''Marvel Team-Up'' #39-40 (November–December 1975), ''The Spectacular Spider-Man'' #19-20 (June–July 1978), ''Dazzler'' #7-8 (October–September 1981), ''Marvel Team-Up'' #138 (February 1984), ''Tales of the Marvels: Inner Demons'' #1 (1996), ''Civil War: War Crimes'' #1 (February 2007), ''Daredevil'' #99-100 (September–October 2007), #102 (January 2008), and ''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #562-563 (August 2008).

Montana appeared as part of the "Enforcers" entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #4.

==Fictional character biography==
Jackson Brice was born in [[Bozeman, Montana]]. Along with [[Fancy Dan]] (Daniel Brito) and [[Ox (comics)|Ox]] (Raymond Bloch), he was a founding member of the [[Enforcers (comics)|Enforcers]].{{Issue|date=January 2011}} He has great proficiency with the [[lasso|lariat]].

Montana, Fancy Dan and Ox make their first appearance under the employ of the [[Frederick Foswell|Big Man]] (Frederick Foswell). During this time, they have their first run-in with their longtime nemesis [[Spider-Man]]. During their first fight against the web-slinger, Montana's lasso skills initially prove successful but Spider-Man defeats the trio.{{Issue|date=May 2011}}

Over the next couple of years, Montana and the team would be employed by [[Lightmaster]] in one of his many schemes, again bringing them into conflict with Spider-Man, with similar results.{{Issue|date=May 2011}} They would then lend their services to Tech-Master in his revenge plot against Harry S. Osgood, only to be defeated by Dazzler.{{Issue|date=May 2011}} Montana and the team would also go up against [[She-Hulk]] at one point.{{Issue|date=May 2011}}

===Post-Civil War===
Montana alongside [[Ox (comics)|Ox]] (Ronald Bloch) and Fancy Dan came back together to work for [[Mister Fear]], which pitted them directly against [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]].{{Issue|date=January 2011}} After Mister Fear's arrest, the Enforcers are taken in by the [[Hood (comics)|Hood]]'s organization.{{Issue|date=January 2011}}

===Brand New Day===
Following the events of ''[[Spider-Man: Brand New Day]]'', the Enforcers are patrons at the [[Bar With No Name]]. they take bets with a person calling himself "The Bookie", over whether Spider-Man will show up to battle "Basher", an unknown villain who claimed to have fought Spider-Man. Spider-Man shows up, but is revealed to be a fake when the real web-head shows up. The Enforcers decide to get revenge on The Bookie, capturing him. The Bookie's father calls Spider-Man for assistance, and he agrees to help. Spider-Man defeats Fancy Dan and Montana.{{Issue|date=January 2011}}

===Big Time===
In the story arc "Kill To Be You", Montana is the right-hand man of the [[Kingpin (comics)|Kingpin]] (Wilson Fisk), and shows contempt toward the [[Phil Urich|Hobgoblin]] (Phil Urich). When Spider-Man and the [[Black Cat (comics)|Black Cat]] come to steal back the Reverbium, Montana falls out a window to his death while Hobgoblin saves Kingpin. Hobgoblin cruelly joked that "he'll be missed by everyone - except the pavement".<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #651</ref>

===Dead No More: The Clone Conspiracy===
During the ''[[Dead No More: The Clone Conspiracy]]'' storyline, Montana was among the cloned villains by [[Jackal (Marvel Comics)|Miles Warren]]'s company New U Technologies.<ref>''Clone Conspiracy'' #2</ref> He was involved in a fight with the other cloned supervillains until it was broken up by the [[Prowler (comics)|Prowler]] (Hobie Brown's clone).<ref>''Prowler'' Vol. 2 #1</ref>

==Other versions==
===Earth X===
In the ''[[Earth X]]'' reality, Montana had mutated where he developed the ability to change his hands into lassos. He and his fellow Enforcers would be hired as heavies to protect President [[Norman Osborn]]. This protection proved to be ineffectual when the Skull came to New York to take over America. He took control of Montana and the others and then murdered Osborn. Montana's fate following the defeat of the Skull remain unrevealed.<ref>''Earth X'' #9</ref>

===Marvel Noir===
Montana and the other Enforcers are muscle for crime boss known as the Goblin.<ref>''Spider-Man Noir'' #1</ref>

===Ultimate Marvel===
The [[Ultimate Marvel]] incarnation of Montana is '''Montana Bale''', an employee of the [[Kingpin (comics)|Kingpin of Crime]] in New York along with [[Fancy Dan]] (Dan Crenshaw) and [[Ox (comics)|Ox]] (Bruno Sanchez).<ref>''Ultimate Spider Man'' #10</ref>

===Film===
* Jackson Brice appears in ''[[Spider-Man: Homecoming]]'', portrayed by [[Logan Marshall-Green]].<ref>https://www.comicbookmovie.com/spider-man/homecoming/spoilers-spider-man-homecoming-cast-list-confirms-jennifer-connelly-and-more-a151972</ref> He is an employee of [[Vulture (Marvel Comics)|Adrian Toomes]]'s salvaging company along with [[Shocker (comics)|Herman Schultz]], [[Tinkerer|Phineas Mason]], and [[Darter (comics)|Randy Vale]]. When their company is shut down following [[Damage Control (comics)|Damage Control]]'s formation, he turned to crime with the rest of his co-workers. He nicknames himself the Shocker and uses a modified version of [[Crossbones (comics)|Crossbones]]' gauntlet that emits vibro-blasts. Brice and Schultz attempt to sell [[Chitauri|retrofitted]] [[Ultron|technology]] to gangster [[Prowler (comics)|Aaron Davis]] when Spider-Man disrupts the transaction. After fending off Spider-Man and returning to the hideout with Schultz, Toomes chastises Brice for risking their operation but Brice dismissively shrugs off the criticism, prompting Toomes to fire him. Brice threatens to expose everything to the authorities and Toomes’ [[Doris Raxton|wife]]. Toomes tries to use an anti-gravity gun to scare Brice, however, mistakingly uses a disintegration weapon instead and kills Brice when firing it. Toomes then passes on Brice's shock glove to Schultz.
