'''Isaac Javitz''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. He was one of the [[Acolytes (comics)|Acolytes]], who first appeared in ''[[Uncanny X-Men]]'' #300.

==Fictional character biography==
Javitz is a [[gigantism|giant]] with enormous strength and durability, who is missing his right eye. Javitz is one of the Acolytes who helped kidnap geneticist [[Moira MacTaggert]]. The group keep her in their current headquarters in a facility in France. Javitz says he's been watching the [[X-Men]] pursue their cause for "years" before throwing himself in the fray. When they battle the X-Men, he seems particularly contemptful of [[Cyclops (Marvel Comics)|Cyclops]], whom he tries to kill. The X-Men ultimately defeat the Acolytes and rescue Moira.<ref>''[[Uncanny X-Men]]'' #300 (May 1993)</ref> With a group of Acolytes, he battles [[X-Factor (comics)|X-Factor]].<ref>''X-Factor'' #92 (July 1993)</ref> With the other Acolytes, Javitz leaves [[Fabian Cortez]] thanks to [[Exodus (comics)|Exodus]], and rejoin [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]]; they appear at [[Magik (comics)|Illyana]]'s funeral, where they recruit [[Colossus (comics)|Colossus]].<ref>''Uncanny X-Men'' #304 (Sep 1993)</ref> The X-Men attack Avalon, and Javitz assists the Acolytes in defending it.<ref>''X-Men'' vol. 2 #25 (Oct 1993)</ref>

[[Omega Red]] attacks the Tyuratum Space Center, thinking they have the cure for his virus, and [[Cable (comics)|Cable]] arrives with a group of the Acolytes under a truce a few hours later. [[Milan (comics)|Milan]] finds the center's director and reads his memories, learning that Omega Red found what he wanted, and the Acolytes tell Cable that Omega Red will need to go into space to administer the cure. They travel to Magneto's old Arctic base to teleport to Avalon, but Cable finds that Omega Red has already been captured at the base by the Acolytes, who reveal the trap they have laid for Cable as well.<ref>''Cable'' Vol. 1 #10 (April 1994)</ref> Cable is able to stop the Acolytes from using Omega Red in their plan to release his virus as a shield to keep humans from getting to Avalon.<ref>''Cable'' Vol. 1 #11 (May 1994)</ref> Javitz is later present at the trial of the [[Neophyte (comics)|Neophyte]], where Colossus is forced out of the Acolytes.<ref>''Uncanny X-Men'' #315 (Aug 1994)</ref>

Javitz is present with the Acolytes and X-Men when the universe is destroyed and replaced with the [[Age of Apocalypse]].<ref>''X-Men'' vol. 2 #41 (Feb 1995)</ref> The Age of Apocalypse universe ends, and the mainstream universe is restored.<ref>''X-Men Prime'' one-shot (July 1995)</ref> The mutant [[Holocaust (Marvel Comics)|Holocaust]] survives his alternate universe's destruction and somehow appears in the mainstream universe when it is restored; he is brought on board the space station [[Providence (comics)|Avalon]], the homebase of the Acolytes. Holocaust awakens and consumes Milan and [[Rusty Collins]].<ref>''X-Men'' vol. 2 #42 (July 1995)</ref> After a running battle, Holocaust kills Javitz as well.<ref>''[[X-Men]]'' v2 #43 (1995)</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Javitz has superhuman strength and durability.
