'''Sligguth''' is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Sligguth was first mentioned in ''[[Marvel Premiere]]'' #4 (September 1972); the character actually first appeared in ''Marvel Premiere'' #5 (November 1972), and was created by [[Archie Goodwin (comics)|Archie Goodwin]] and [[Barry Windsor-Smith]]. Sligguth was apparently destroyed in ''Marvel Premiere'' #6 (January 1973). Sligguth's "birth" was detailed in ''Silver Surfer Annual'' #2 (1989).

==Fictional character biography==
Sligguth is a [[snake]]-like [[List of Marvel Comics demons|demon]], the spawn of [[Father Set|Set]], who has clashed with [[Doctor Strange]]. He works closely with his "dark priestess" Ebora and is served by an army of [[Serpent Men]]. Sligguth was apparently destroyed during a fight with Strange.<ref>''Marvel Premiere'' #6</ref>

Sligguth's history was revealed sometime later: Set spawned several offspring, one of which was Sligguth, shortly after the formation of the Earth and the creation of the Elder Gods. Set and his offspring later abandoned their physical forms and escaped to another dimension, allowing the [[Atum (comics)|Demogorge]] to destroy their physical forms as part of his destruction of the Elder Gods.<ref>''Silver Surfer Annual'' #2 (1989)</ref>

During the ''[[Fear Itself (comics)|Fear Itself]]'' storyline, Sligguth attended the Devil's Advocacy where they discussed the [[Serpent (comics)|Serpent]]'s threat on Earth.<ref>''Journey Into Mystery'' #627</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Sligguth has super-strength and enhanced durability.
