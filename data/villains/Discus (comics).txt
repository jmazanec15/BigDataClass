'''Discus''' ('''Tim Stuart''') is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Discus first appeared in ''Power Man'' #16 in December 1974, and was created by [[Tony Isabella]] and [[Billy Graham (comics)|Billy Graham]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Power Man'' #22 (December 1974), ''Power Man and Iron Fist'' #50 (April 1978), ''Iron Man'' #126-127 (Septemberâ€“October 1979), ''Power Man and Iron Fist'' #110 (October 1984), ''Spectacular Spider-Man Annual'' #12 (1992), ''Web of Spider-Man Annual'' #8 (1992), ''New Warriors Annual'' #2 (1992), ''Civil War: War Crimes'' (February 2007), and ''Amazing Spider-Man'' #562-563 (August 2008).

==Fictional character biography==
The youngest son of Tyler Stuart, a warden at Seagate prison, Tim Stuart was employed by [[Justin Hammer]] and given a costume, jet-pack, and assorted weaponry. He took the name Discus, as his weapon of choice was a throwing disc; he usually carried disc-shaped flying blades. The younger brother of [[Stiletto (comics)|Stiletto]], he was most often partnered, and rarely went on his own missions. Like Stiletto, he was a constant thorn in [[Luke Cage]]'s side, but he also battled the [[Heroes for Hire]], the [[Daughters of the Dragon]], and [[Iron Fist (comics)|Iron Fist]].<ref>''Power Man'' #22</ref><ref>''Power Man and Iron Fist'' #50</ref> Much later, both Stiletto and Discus were seen in Justin Hammer's employ. Attempting to capture [[Iron Man]], Stiletto and Discus attacked but were easily defeated after their weapons were unable to harm him.<ref>''Iron Man'' #126-127</ref> He and his brother, while imprisoned, found religion, but soon reverted to their old ways and began working for [[Nightshade (Marvel Comics)|Deadly Nightshade]]. However, as Stiletto continued on in his criminal career, Discus has not been seen for quite some time.<ref>''Power Man and Iron Fist'' #110</ref>

A partnership began with the [[Sphinx (comics)|Sphinx]] and Justin Hammer brought Discus back to villainy. Along with Hammer's villain army, Discus battled [[Nova (Richard Rider)|Nova]] and [[Namorita]], but he was defeated by both of them.<ref>''Spectacular Spider-Man Annual'' #12</ref><ref>''Web of Spider-man Annual'' #8</ref>

Discus returned and is recruited as part of [[Hammerhead (comics)|Hammerhead]]'s supervillain army. However, [[Iron Man]] and [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] forces attacked their forces.<ref>''[[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]]: War Crimes'' #1</ref>

Discus appeared in ''[[Spider-Man: Brand New Day|Brand New Day]]'' as one of the villains at the Bar with No Name.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'' #562-563 (August 2008)</ref>
