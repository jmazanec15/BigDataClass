===Fictional character biography===
'''Maximillian Zaran''' was born in [[Great Britain]].  Early in his life, he was an agent of the British Secret Service: MI-6, but soon chose to become a mercenary and assassin. Training himself thoroughly in martial arts and the use of various kinds of weapons, he became known as a super-villain as well. He was first employed by Sarsfield and then Fah Lo Suee, and his first battle against a super-hero was against [[Shang-Chi]], the Master of Kung-Fu, who easily defeated him.<ref>''Master of Kung Fu'' #77-79</ref> He later battled and defeated Shang-Chi.<ref>''Master of Kung Fu'' #87</ref> Zaran then joined [[Batroc the Leaper|Batroc]]'s Brigade, and was employed by [[Obadiah Stane]] to steal [[Captain America]]'s [[Captain America's shield|shield]].<ref>''Captain America'' #302-303</ref>

In his next attempt to defeat super-heroes, Zaran joined forces with [[Razor Fist|Razorfist II]] and [[Shockwave (comics)|Shockwave]] and fought the [[West Coast Avengers]] [[Hawkeye (comics)|Hawkeye]], [[Mockingbird (Marvel Comics)|Mockingbird]], and [[Iron Man]].<ref>''West Coast Avengers'' #11</ref> He battled Hawkeye again alongside Batroc's Brigade.<ref>''Solo Avengers'' #3</ref> 

During the Bloodstone Hunt, Zaran became good friends with fellow mercenary Batroc. Batroc's Brigade was employed by [[Baron Zemo#Heinrich Zemo|Baron Zemo]] to acquire the fragments of the Bloodstone, and Zaran fought Captain America and [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]] but lost once more.<ref>''Captain America'' #357-362</ref> The brigade was later hired by [[Maelstrom (comics)|Maelstrom]] to help him build a device that could destroy the universe and battled the [[Great Lakes Avengers]], during which he killed the newly initiated G.L.A. member [[Grasshopper (comics)#Doug Taggert|Grasshopper]].<ref>''GLA: Misassembled'' #1-4 (2005)</ref>

====Marvel Knights====
Zaran is employed by the Shadow-Hand to steal a chemical elixir from [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]] for Shang-Chi's father, a super-villain sometimes known as [[Fu Manchu]]. He is then ordered to kill Shang-Chi himself.<ref>''Master of Kung Fu'' #1</ref>

===Powers and abilities===
Zaran is an athletic man with no superhuman powers. He received British Secret Service training, and has knowledge of all forms of armed and unarmed combat, and extensive knowledge of ancient and modern weapons, including knives, bows, staffs, maces, spears, nunchakus, shuriken, and guns. He wears a leather outfitted with a variety of specialized clips, loops, and pockets for carrying weapons. He usually carries small sais (three pronged daggers) attached to his gauntlets, collar and codpiece, a bo staff/spear/blow gun, and a wide variety of weapons as needed.

The second Zaran was trained by the original Zaran to become a mercenary. The second Zaran sought out Shang-Chi in battle but was defeated.<ref>''[[Master of Kung Fu]]: Bleeding Black'' #1 (1990)</ref>

==Other versions==

===House of M===
In this reality, Zaran is a member of [[Shang-Chi]]'s Dragons criminal organization, alongside, [[Mantis (Marvel Comics)|Mantis]], [[Swordsman (comics)|Swordsman]] and [[Machete (comics)|Machete]].<ref>''House of M: Avengers'' #2</ref>  Zaran is killed by Bullseye after the Dragons are ambushed by the Kingpin's assassins.<ref>''House of M: Avengers'' #4</ref>
