The '''Great Gambonnos''' are [[fictional characters]]; two [[twin]] [[supervillain]]s appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[Steve Ditko]], the Great Gambonnos first appeared in ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man]]'' #16 in (September 1964).  The characters subsequently appear in ''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #22 (March 1965), ''The Avengers'' #22 (November 1965), ''The Amazing Spider-Man Annual'' #2 (1965), ''Thor'' #145-147 (October–December 1967), ''Super-Villain Team-Up'' #8 (October 1976), ''Ghost Rider'' #72-73 (September–October 1982), ''The Sensational She-Hulk'' #1 (May 1989), ''X-Men and Power Pack'' #3 (February 2006), and ''She-Hulk'' #5 (April 2006). They also appear as "The Flying Gambannos" in ''[[Dr. Doom and the Masters of Evil]]'' #2 (February 2009). The characters were also part of the "Circus of Crime" entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #2.

==Fictional character biography==
'''Ernesto and Luigi Gambonno''' were born in [[Milan, Italy]]. They are [[acrobatics|acrobat]]s and aerialists working for the criminal organization, the [[Circus of Crime]]. They are identical [[twin]]s and though they perceive themselves as the "Kings of the High Trapeze", they are defeated pretty easily by [[Spider-Man]] and [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]] who prove to be superior acrobats to the both of them.<ref>''[[The Amazing Spider-Man]]'' #16 (Sep. 1964)</ref>

Frustrated by this defeat, the Gambonnos are more than willing to throw in with the [[Clown (comics)|Clown]], [[Princess Python]], and [[Human Cannonball (Marvel Comics)|Cannonball]].{{Issue|date=January 2010}} They kick the Ringmaster out and become the Masters of Menace, a term coined by Princess Python.{{Issue|date=January 2010}} They steal some valuable paintings. When Spider-Man attacks the Masters at their hideout, the Gambonnos swing from their set of gymnastic rings, but they are clobbered by the web-slinger anyway and sent to jail.{{Issue|date=January 2010}} They rejoin the Ringmaster when he tries to lure [[Hawkeye (comics)|Hawkeye]], [[Quicksilver (comics)|Quicksilver]] and the [[Scarlet Witch]] into the group, but cannot defeat Quicksilver.{{Issue|date=January 2010}}

The Gambonnos -- alongside [[Ringmaster (comics)|Ringmaster]] and the [[Clown (comics)|Clown]])-- were among the villains in [[Hammerhead (comics)|Hammerhead]]'s unnamed villain army when [[Iron Man]] and [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] raided their hideout.<ref>Civil War: War Crimes #1 (February 2007)</ref> During the "Opening Salvo" part of the ''[[Secret Empire (comics)|Secret Empire]]'' storyline, the Great Gambonnos were with the Circus of Crime while being recruited by [[Helmut Zemo|Baron Helmut Zemo]] to join the Army of Evil.<ref>''Captain America: Steve Rogers'' #16</ref>

The Gambonnos later appear robbing an armored truck until they're stopped by [[Moon Girl (Marvel Comics)|Moon Girl]] and [[Devil Dinosaur]], along with the teenage [[Cyclops (Marvel Comics)|Cyclops]] and [[Nova (Sam Alexander)|Nova]].<ref>''Champions'' Vol.2 #16</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Ernesto and Luigi Gambonno have no superpowers, but they are expert acrobats and aerialists.

==In other media==
